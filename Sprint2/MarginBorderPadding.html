HTML/CSS Margin, Border and padding

My friend Google said that in web design, margin creates space outside an element, separating it from other elements, while padding creates space inside an element, between its content and its border, and a border is the visible or invisible line around the edge of the box.

CSS HTML Box Model

Here's a more detailed breakdown:

Margin - The space between an element and other elements on the page. Think of it as the "outer" space, like the gap between two picture frames on a wall. Margins are transparent, meaning you'll see the background color or image behind them. Margins can be negative, allowing for overlapping elements.

Padding - The space between an element's content and its border. Think of it as the "inner" space, like the matting around a picture in a frame. Padding takes on the element's background color or image. Padding cannot be negative.

Border - The visible or invisible line around the edge of an element. You can control the style (solid, dashed, dotted, etc.), width, and color of a border. Think of it as the frame around a picture.

Difference between margin and padding in CSS?

Some people confuse margin with padding, because both terms mean spacing and neighbour with border. However, they have principle differences and right term operation can save you much time. Margin is an external element, which means it exists outside the box. It controls the spacing between different elements (boxes) and between the element (box) with the edge of the screen. Padding, on the contrary, determines inside spacing, which can control how much the background color is visible around the content.
