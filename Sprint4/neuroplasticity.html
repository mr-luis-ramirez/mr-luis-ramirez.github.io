Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity as refer by some as a fancy medical term to describe a person’s brain ability to learn and adapt. It somehow a rewiring process that allows our mind to grow and adjust with new and it’s increasing demands. It is also the brain’s skill to adapt in response to experiences, learning and even brain damage.

This adaptation can involve:

  • Synaptic Plasticity - Strengthening or weakening connections between neurons (synapses).
  • Neurogenesis - The creation of new neurons.
  • Reorganization - The brain rewiring itself to compensate for damage or learn new skills.

Factors the influence Neuroplasticity are as follows:

  • Learning and Experience - Doing new activities, acquiring new skills, and gaining knowledge can stimulate neuroplasticity.
  • Brain Injury - After a stroke or traumatic brain injury, the brain can restructure itself to recuperate lost functions.
  • Stress - Chronic stress can negatively influence neuroplasticity.

Final word

As previous belief that the brain only develop on the early stage of life is now being corrected as William James presented the first theory of neuroplasticity, suggesting that the human brain is capable of reorganizing, In the book "Principle of Psychology" written over 100 years ago. It was not until the mid to latter half of the 20th century, following a wide range of research which showed that many aspects of the brain remain changeable even into adulthood, that the term Neuroplasticity came to prominence.

Latest research shows that brain development and behaviour are guided by a basic genetic blueprint, in addition to a range of experiences and all that shapes the emerging brain. As we age, the rate of change in the brain, or neuroplasticity, declines but does not come to a halt. In addition, we now know that new neurons can appear in certain parts of the brain up until the day we die. Neuroplasticity can be manipulated in both the healthy and diseased brain. Utilising the brains' ability to create and lay down new pathways the physiotherapist can play a big role in rehabilitation and improved quality of life. Further study suggest that brain can be trained and developed, for brain that has been damage or have declined due to old age.
