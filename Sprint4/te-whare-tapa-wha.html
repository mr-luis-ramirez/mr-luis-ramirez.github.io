Te-whare-tapa-wha

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model compose of four dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 that present a Māori viewpoint on health.

The 4 dimensions are:

Taha tinana (physical wellbeing)

How your body feels and how you look after it. Replenishing your body lets you to feel mentally well.

Taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)

Looks at your mind, heart, conscience, thoughts and feelings. It’s concerning what you feel, as well as the way you convey and think.

Taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

The way people view wairua can be very different. Some would say that this is the ability for faith or religious convictions or having a belief in a higher power. For others, wairua is an internal relationship to the universe or the holy.

Taha whānau (family wellbeing)

This is about who makes you feel like you fit in, who you care about and who you share your life with. It could be the extended relationships, may not always your immediate relatives, it’s your friends, hoamahi (colleagues), community and the individuals you care about.



Wall Activity
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Doing a 5-minute brisk walk going to work
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Taking a eat out with my partner once a week
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Reading a motivational or mental exercise every Sunday
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Praying or thanking the Above for every day blessing
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Thanking a plant or tree every time I take a shade om them
