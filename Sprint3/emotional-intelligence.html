Emotional Intelligence

EQ (Emotional Quotient) vs IQ (Intelligent Quotient)

When comparing EQ (Emotional Quotient) to IQ (Intelligent Quotient), the fundamental differences between these two types of intelligence, would be that IQ primarily measures intellectual abilities such as logical thinking, problem-solving, and analytical abilities, EQ refers to your aptitude for knowing and handling of emotions, commiserating with others, and building effective interpersonal relationships.

Is IQ or EQ an inborn trait?

There will always be a debate about nature versus nurture, but e Evidence suggests that both IQ and EQ have a significant genetic element. Research have found a strong heritability factor in cognitive abilities, implying you can be born with varying degrees of intellectual ability. Similarly, study has also unveiled genes associated with emotional processing and parameter. This suggests some people are more likely to gain emotional intelligence personality.

On the other hand, environmental factors such as childhood, education, and life incidents significantly shape both IQ and EQ throughout your life. Therefore, while you might naturally gravitate toward either intelligence type, the relationship between genetics and environment ultimately determines your cognitive and emotional capabilities.

Practice Mindfulness

Emphasis on the present moment by paying attention to your breath, body feelings, or settings without judgment, and gently redirect your thought when your mind wanders. Working mindfulness involves breathing methods, directed imagery, and other practices to relax the body and mind and help ease stress.

There are many simple ways to practice mindfulness. Some examples include:

  • Pay attention
  • Live in the moment
  • Accept yourself
  • Focus on your breathing

Meditation for mindfulness

The general evidence boosts the effectiveness of meditation for various conditions, including:

  • Stress
  • Anxiety
  • Pain
  • High blood pressure (hypertension)

Meditation also has been shown to

  • Improve attention
  • Decrease job burnout
  • Improve sleep
  • Improve diabetes control
